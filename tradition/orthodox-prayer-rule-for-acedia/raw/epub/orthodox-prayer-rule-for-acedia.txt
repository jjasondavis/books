ORTHODOX PRAYER RULE FOR ACEDIA (LAY RULE)
This prayer rule is fixed, modest, and sustainable. Its purpose is stability, presence, and healing of acedia (restless dissatisfaction).
PRINCIPLES • Shorter beats longer • Daily beats intense • Completion beats inspiration • Stillness beats insight • Fidelity beats optimization
MORNING RULE (10–15 minutes) Before phone or email:
1. Trisagion Prayers 2. Psalm 50 (51) 3. One fixed Morning Prayer 4. Jesus Prayer (10–25 repetitions) 5. Concluding Prayer
MIDDAY ANCHOR (1–2 minutes) Once during the workday: Jesus Prayer (5–10 repetitions)
EVENING RULE (10–15 minutes) Before bed:
1. Trisagion Prayers 2. Psalm 90 (91) or Psalm 4 3. Brief examination (no analysis) 4. Prayer of St. Ephraim 5. Jesus Prayer (10 repetitions) 6. Sign of the Cross
WEEKLY STRUCTURE • Saturday: slightly longer evening prayer • Sunday: lighter rule • Regular confession • Simple, consistent fasting
WHAT NOT TO DO • Do not chase feelings • Do not expand the rule • Do not abandon it when dry • Do not optimize endlessly • Do not use prayer to solve problems
FINAL NOTE Prayer is not for insight, but alignment.
