RICH JESUS, POOR JESUS
"For you know the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, that though he was rich, yet for your sake he became poor, so that you through his poverty might become rich."--2 Corinthians 8:9
HOW DO YOU SEE JESUS? Was he some poor struggling carpenter, walking around the Judean countryside attempting to create a new religion? Or was he the wealthy son of a heavenly potentate with riches vaster than anything we've ever known before?
How you answer the previous questions will determine in large part how you approach the subject of prosperity.
When I got started in Christianity, I was introduced to the poor Jesus. He was presented to me as a struggling messiah, misunderstood, rejected and basically outcast.
But as I began to grow in my faith and understanding of the scriptures, I began noticing something quite different. This new version of Jesus I encountered wasn't the poor struggling guy I originally thought. This Jesus was a powerfully wealthy, stupendously luxuriant conquering King. He has wealth so vast that he commissioned the building of a city where actual gold is used to pave the streets with--like the way we use basic asphalt here on our earthly cities!
Jesus is so opulent with his wealth that gold and precious stones are used in place of bricks and mortar. This version of Jesus isn't opposed to wealth. In fact, he's dripping with it!
But we rarely if ever get presented THIS image of Jesus. Instead, we are left with the impression that Jesus was so poor he couldn't afford a place to lay his head.

JESUS CHRIST THE MILLIONAIRE?
Was Jesus Christ a Millionaire? That depends on how you choose to look at it. If we are comparing the standards of wealth based on 2000-year-old Hebrew customs to modern day standards of wealth, there's a lot of evidence that supports the idea of Jesus Christ having been a man of means--at least in the early part of his life.
When he was born, Jesus was presented with gifts of wealth befitting a king. And not just any king: but the greatest king ever born. We often think 3 old men brought little boxes of gold, frankincense, and myrrh to Jesus.
But let's look a little closer. Legend has it that the men known as the wise men or magi were men from the Persian Empire. They studied the teachings and writings of the prophet Daniel, who was a Hebrew captive later turned advisor to several kings during the years when Israel was in captivity. One of those kings was the King of Persia.
Daniel understood the arts and science of the Chaldeans. Part of that science included the ability to forecast future events based on observation of stars, planets, and other celestial bodies. Daniel, whose wisdom was given to him by God, was able to understand and interpret what we today would call occult astrology and stargazing.
Being a captive of an occult King, God just gave Daniel MORE knowledge in this area than the others he served with. And based on these star interpretations, Daniel foresaw the coming of the Jew's Messiah: the future King of Kings who would rule the world, restore Israel to its place of glory, and restore God's way of doing things to universal mankind.
1 | Page


Daniel--we are told from reading the bible--was given much wealth by not only Nebuchadnezzar, but also by his grandson King Belshazzar. And later by Darius the King of Persia when he conquered Babylon.
According to legend, Daniel saved up his money, being a eunuch; and since he had no children to bequeath his wealth to, he left strict instructions that his wealth be preserved intact and given as an offering to the King of Kings who was yet to be born, according to the astrological sign in the stars.
The magi--who came to the young Jesus after his birth-- were men who studied under the school of learning set up by Daniel. They were men who were taught to expect the appearing of a certain star at a specific time, during a certain year.
And when this star appeared, it would signal the birth of the greatest King the world would ever know. This was the star that appeared in the east at the time of Jesus' birth, which when followed by the wise men, led them to Jesus in Israel. It took them 2 years just to make that journey.
Now about these wise men: who said it was only 3 men? I think people get the number 3, from the 3 gifts that were presented: gold, frankincense, and myrrh. More than likely, it was well over 3 men who came. Why? Because the wise men traveled with a caravan. In those days, people often traveled in caravans to avoid being robbed by bandits who hid along the paths people traveled on at night.
These weren't just any type of men. These were WISE men; and they would have known to not travel alone bearing large amounts of gold and precious spices. They were extremely important highly esteemed men. And they came bringing wealth befitting a great king. So, most likely they traveled in

a huge caravan containing over a hundred men: including private armed guards from their own country.
Many years earlier when King Solomon sat on the throne as ruler over Israel, he was presented with a gift by an African queen known only as the Queen of the South. She too came in a huge caravan to present a gift to the wise King of Israel: a gift with a monetary value worth over $600,000,000 when adjusted for modern inflation.
The wise men who came to see Jesus knew they were coming to witness a king's birth who in their minds was GREATER than King Solomon. So their gifts of gold, frankincense and myrrh were reflective of their respect for this awesome young king.
I mention all of this to show that it's very probable that Jesus came into this world a very rich kid. Though born in an animal stable due to circumstances, he was extremely wealthy in material things shortly afterwards. And why wouldn't he be? Would the greatest king ever born have anything less than the absolute best? There's absolutely nothing wrong with that.
If God is to do exceeding abundant things for you, you must first develop an exceeding abundance mindset. You must expand your capacity to think bigger and receive bigger.
God desires to do more for all of us. But often times, He simply can't. It's that whole free will thing. If you're not willing, God won't force it on you. Expand your desire and capacity to receive greater. Start today. Open your mind to ideas of greater expressions of wealth.
It's your divine right to prosper.

AMBITION TO SUCCEED IS GOOD IN THE EYES OF GOD
One of the secrets of the biblical patriarch Jacob's success was his determination to succeed.
Jacob desperately wanted to succeed in life. He wasn't his father's favorite son. His brother Esau was.
Esau came out of the womb first--even though they were twins. According to the law of the firstborn son, Esau would be given a double portion of all that Isaac had accumulated over the years: including the favor and blessing of God.

But God wanted Jacob to get that blessing. So, God orchestrated circumstances to befall the brothers, in such a manner, that Esau sold his firstborn birthright to his twin brother for a bowl of stew meat. And God honored the sale.
Imagine selling a lifetime of God's favor over your life--for a bowl of food! That's how little regard Esau had for the spiritual things of God.
Meanwhile, Jacob wanted the blessings of God so badly in his life, that he was willing to deny his famished brother some food to have it.
>> What was so important about birth rights in the ancient world?
For one, the birthright carried with it a double portion of the father's inheritance. THIS MEANT MONEY: tangible physical wealth--gold--silver--coinage--servants--cattle--livestock.
Many believers have been deceived into believing that God doesn't want you to care about earthly tangible wealth. The Book of Genesis greatly dispels this myth.
God wants believers to have money in abundance. He'd rather the economies and financial systems of this present world be in the hands of His people than the Devil's people.
But in order for this to happen, Christians need to learn what God really wants to accomplish in the earth today--and become more ambitious in seeing that happen. And here's a hint: it's much more than just saving souls.
Jacob was incredibly ambitious. That's why he won the birthright. And it's why God had no problem prospering him in all of his affairs.

WHAT'S IN YOUR HAND?
Your prosperity in life isn't predicated on the goodness of God nor His mercy. It's predicated on your willingness to take what's in your hand, RIGHT NOW, and allow God to turbocharge it for maximum effect.
Too many people think they can guilt-trip God into showing them enough mercy to get them out of poverty or some financial trouble. In case you haven't noticed: that approach rarely works. Because the truth is that God isn't the one who's holding you back: YOU ARE!
You are the only one withholding your own good from being manifested. God has literally blessed every human being on this planet with enough inner wealth to be easily translated into external material wealth. There's a means and a method to doing this, which is what this book is all about. But it can be done once the mind is made up to do it.
When Moses stood before the burning bush, chatting it up with God, he had doubts about the mission God was sending him on. He lacked the skills, knowledge, the power, the ARMY to force the hand of the mighty Pharaoh. God looked at Moses and asked Him a simple yet profound question...one I now ask you to consider too:
"WHAT'S THAT IN YOUR HAND?"
That one simple question changed the course of Moses' life. That day he became Israel's liberator. Recognition of the thing that's in your hand is the key to getting out of your current situation. All Moses had in his hand was a stick. He used it to drive cattle and sheep with, and to fight off predators when necessary. It was the simple tools of the trade that any shepherd used in his simple line of work.

There was nothing special about Moses' stick. That is, until God asked him to seriously consider it.
The moment Moses became aware that God can take something as simple as his staff and turn it into a tool of power and deliverance, became the moment Moses started stepping into his bigger destiny.
Whatever's in your hand today, no matter how small or insignificant you think it is: God can empower it to not only get you out of debt, but to KEEP you out of debt by prospering you beyond your wildest dreams.
What's in your hand?
I challenge you to carefully consider it, and give it up to God. Have a prayer of submission where you literally take your 'staff or stick,' and ask God to bless it for battle.
David had a slingshot and five rocks when he went up against the mighty Goliath. Not a weapon of mass destruction by a long shot. Yet, supercharged by the awesome power of God, that sling shot was as good as a cocked and loaded .357 magnum in David's hand. It got the job done. And David became a mighty warrior that day. All because he recognized what was in his hand, and allowed God to use it to a greater effect.
