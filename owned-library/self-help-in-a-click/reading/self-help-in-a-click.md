-- CHAPTER 1 -- SEND EMAIL
BY DAVID RIKLAN

We all do it.

Some of us do it at night while sitting all by ourselves. Some of us do it in a room filled with other people. Some of us do it during lunch. Some people do it in bed. Some people even do it while driving. Some of us do it to one person at a time. Some of us do it to three or four or even hundreds of people at a time.
Even my mother does it.

We all send email. Sending email is the number-one application on the Internet.

** Email penetration is at an all-time high of 91 percent among Internet users between the ages of 18 and 64, as reported by a Pew Internet & American Life Project survey.

I am sure almost everybody knows the value of sending out email for business and for your personal life. Try to imagine your life without email today. In order to make your email usage even more life-changing, please see my top tips for email effectiveness.

TOP TIPS FOR SENDING EMAIL

1.

Write a clear and useful subject line.

2.

Never assume the messages you are sending are private. Your

email can easily be forwarded to other people.

3.

Re-read your message before sending it. Once you press the

send button, it is gone.

4.

Try to respond promptly to email messages sent to you.

5.

Try to keep the message clearly focused and easy to read.

6.

Use the same etiquette that you'd use to communicate with a

person by the phone or in person.

7.

Use a different writing style for formal and informal situations.

8.

When you reply to an email message, you should include that

message, but only as much as is necessary to establish the

context.

9.

Do not write all of your emails in CAPS. It is called shouting

and isn't appropriate.

10. Check with the recipient of your email before you send them a

huge attachment.

11. Know the difference between To:, CC:, and BCC: and use them

appropriately. To: is who gets the email. CC: is Carbon Copy,

and BCC: is Blind Carbon Copy.

12. Set up a separate email account for lower priority messages. It's

reasonable to have two or more email accounts and use them

for different things.

13. Double check to see that you are actually sending the email to

the correct person.

14. Don't have email replace human contact. The phone and in-

person contact are still critical.
