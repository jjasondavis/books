INTRODUCTION

Fight Back Like A Monk: Resisting Temptation With Resolve

Temptation is an inherent part of the human experience, a constant companion that tests our resolve and challenges our faith. Throughout the ages, countless individuals have sought ways to combat these alluring distractions that lead us astray from our spiritual paths. Among the most revered guides in this endeavor are the ancient Christian monks, whose ascetic lives serve as a testament to the power of discipline, faith, and resolve.

One such monk, whose teachings continue to resonate through the centuries, is Evagrius Ponticus. Born in 345 AD, Evagrius was a prominent figure in the early Christian monastic movement. His profound insights into the human soul and the nature of temptation have guided generations of believers in their spiritual journeys. Evagrius's writings, particularly his work on combating tempting thoughts, offer a practical and deeply spiritual approach to overcoming the myriad temptations that beset us daily.

The Legacy of Evagrius Ponticus

Evagrius Ponticus's contributions to Christian thought are invaluable. As a theologian and ascetic, he dedicated his life to understanding the intricacies of the human mind and its vulnerabilities. His most famous work, often referred to as the "Talking Back" or "Antirrhetikos," is a comprehensive guide to the ascetic life. This text, originally written in Greek and subsequently translated into Syriac and Armenian, has been preserved through numerous manuscripts and remains one of the best-preserved of Evagrius's writings.

"Fight Back Like A Monk: Resisting Temptation With Resolve" draws heavily on the wisdom of Evagrius Ponticus. In this book, we delve into 125 tempting thoughts, or "demons," as Evagrius described them, and present the corresponding Bible verses that can be used to combat each one. This compilation serves not only as a spiritual guide but also as a practical tool for those seeking to fortify their minds and spirits against the relentless onslaught of temptation.

The Nature of Temptation

Temptation is more than just the lure of material or sensual pleasures. It encompasses a wide range of thoughts and desires that can divert us from our spiritual goals and lead us into sin. Evagrius identified these tempting thoughts and categorized them into eight general logismoi (thoughts or demons): gluttony, lust, avarice, sadness, anger, acedia (spiritual sloth), vainglory, and pride. Each of these thoughts represents a different facet of the human condition, and each requires a unique approach to overcome.

Evagrius's method for combating these thoughts is rooted in the practice of "talking back" or antirrhesis. This technique involves countering tempting thoughts with specific Bible verses, thus using the power of God's Word to refute and dispel the allure of sin. By internalizing and reciting these verses, believers can strengthen their resolve and maintain their focus on their spiritual journey.

A Practical Guide for Modern Believers

While Evagrius Ponticus lived in a time and culture vastly different from our own, the wisdom of his teachings remains timeless. In "Fight Back Like A Monk," we aim to bridge the gap between the ancient and the modern, providing readers with a practical guide that is both accessible and deeply rooted in the Christian tradition. This book is structured to offer a clear path for those seeking to overcome temptation and cultivate a life of spiritual discipline.

Each chapter focuses on a specific category of tempting thoughts, exploring their nature and impact on our lives. We then present the corresponding Bible verses that Evagrius recommended for combating these thoughts.

The Power of Scripture

The Bible is a wellspring of wisdom and strength for believers. Its verses offer comfort, guidance, and a direct line to God's truth. By incorporating scripture into our daily battles against temptation, we arm ourselves with the most potent weapon available – the Word of God. Evagrius understood this deeply, and his method of "talking back" is a testament to the enduring power of scripture.

In this book, we honor Evagrius's legacy by presenting these Bible verses in a way that is both faithful to his teachings and relevant to contemporary readers. Each verse has been carefully chosen for its ability to counter specific temptations, providing a robust toolkit for spiritual warfare.

Embracing the Ascetic Life

The ascetic life is one of discipline, sacrifice, and deep spiritual commitment. While not everyone is called to the monastic life, the principles of asceticism can be applied to any Christian seeking to deepen their faith and resist temptation. This book encourages readers to adopt an ascetic mindset, embracing practices such as fasting, prayer, and meditation on scripture to strengthen their spiritual resolve.

By following the guidance of Evagrius Ponticus and other early Christian monks, modern believers can cultivate a life of greater spiritual depth and resilience. "Fight Back Like A Monk" is designed to be a companion on this journey, offering insights and practical tools for those committed to resisting temptation and living a life of faith.

"Fight Back Like A Monk: Resisting Temptation With Resolve" is more than just a book; it is a call to action. It invites readers to take up the mantle of spiritual warriors, armed with the wisdom of the ancients and the power of scripture. By understanding and confronting the tempting thoughts that assail us, we can achieve a deeper connection with God and a more steadfast commitment to our spiritual paths.

As you embark on this journey, remember that you are not alone. The teachings of Evagrius Ponticus and the enduring strength of God's Word are with you, guiding and supporting you every step of the way. Together, let us fight back against temptation with the resolve of a monk, forging a path of faith, discipline, and spiritual triumph.

Evagrius of Pontus, born around 345 AD in Pontus (modern-day Turkey), stands as a pivotal figure in early Christian monasticism and theological thought. His life and teachings profoundly influenced the development of Christian spirituality, particularly in the Eastern Christian tradition.

Early Life and Spiritual Formation

Evagrius's early life is somewhat obscure, but he is believed to have received a classical education and served in the Roman army before embracing a monastic vocation. Influenced by the Desert Fathers of Egypt, Evagrius journeyed to the desert seeking spiritual enlightenment and communion with God. This period of asceticism and contemplation became foundational to his theological insights.

Contributions to Christian Theology

Trinitarian Theology: Evagrius contributed to early Christian understanding of the Trinity, emphasizing the unity and distinctiveness of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. His works often delve into the mystical aspects of the Trinity, exploring how divine unity and diversity coalesce in Christian doctrine.

Anthropology and Sin: Central to Evagrius's theology is his understanding of the human condition and the nature of sin. He developed a comprehensive anthropology that integrates Greek philosophical concepts with Christian asceticism, emphasizing the struggle against sin and the purification of the soul.

Prayer and Contemplation: Evagrius is renowned for his teachings on prayer and contemplation, which he viewed as essential practices for spiritual growth and communion with God. His works, such as the Praktikos and the Gnostic, outline practical methods for cultivating a deep, contemplative prayer life rooted in biblical wisdom and ascetic discipline.

Legacy in Christian Spirituality

Evagrius's legacy extends beyond his theological writings. He is credited with systematizing the practice of hesychasm, a form of contemplative prayer that emphasizes inner stillness and the quieting of the mind to experience God's presence. This practice later became foundational in Eastern Orthodox spirituality and contributed to the development of mystical theology.

Controversies and Influence

Despite his profound impact, Evagrius faced controversy in his lifetime, particularly due to his association with Origenist teachings, which were later condemned as heretical. His works on spiritual discernment and the psychology of temptation, however, continued to influence theologians and ascetics throughout the centuries.

PROLOGUE 

I'm writing about how our minds struggle in the world: first, what we fight against; second, who helps us in the fight; and finally, what challenges we face. We humans are the fighters, helped by God's angels, fighting against evil demons.

Failure doesn't happen because our enemies are too strong or because our angels aren't helpful enough. Instead, it's when we aren't prepared that we lose our understanding of God and fail.

Our Lord Jesus Christ gave us power to overcome snakes, scorpions, and the devil. He taught us how to fight evil. When demons attack us with bad thoughts and tempt us to sin, we can use the Bible to stop those thoughts. Sin leads to death, as Ezekiel 18:4 says.

When we think wrong thoughts and act on them, we sin. Solomon said that when people don't face punishment right away for doing bad things, they keep doing them (Ecclesiastes 8:11). He also warned us not to argue with foolish people, or we might act foolish too (Proverbs 26:4).

When someone acts foolishly and gets angry with others, they're acting like the demons who are furious like snakes (Deuteronomy 32:33). But if someone is patient and says, "Don't get angry," (Psalm 37:8), they're wise and show that the demons' tricks are foolish and against God's teachings.

When we try to speak against our enemies, the demons, it's hard to find the right words quickly because they're scattered in the Bible and can be difficult to understand. So, we have carefully selected words from the Bible to fight our battles, like brave soldiers for Jesus Christ, our King.

Remember, the more we stand up to the demons and speak against them, the angrier they are towards us. Job and David also faced this; they spoke peacefully, but the demons attacked them unfairly (Psalm 120:7). We shouldn't be scared of them but should be strong and brave with Jesus on our side.

For if we believe in Christ and keep His commandments, we will cross the Jordan and take the city of the palms. In this battle, we need spiritual weapons, specifically, steadfast faith and doctrine, that is, fasting, virtues, humility, peacefulness and unceasing prayer.

I marvel if anyone can fight the battle in their mind and be crowned with the crown of righteousness while quickly stirring up anger, neglecting prayer, and associating with heretics. For Paul says, 'Everyone who competes in the games exercises self-control in all things' [1 Corinthians 9:25] and shows humility to all people, always lifting up his hands without anger and evil thoughts [1 Timothy 2:8].

Therefore, it is fitting for us, engaging in this spiritual warfare, to clothe ourselves with spiritual armor and show to our opponents that we fight against sin even to the point of shedding blood, overthrowing wicked thoughts and every high thing that exalts itself against the knowledge of God [2 Corinthians 10:5], and striving not to set the man before the platform of Christ, but the mind. For the man who sets himself apart turns away from sin through works, but the mind that sets itself apart turns away from sin through the thoughts of our mind, and in the time of prayer, it sees the light of the Holy Trinity.

But now is the time to fight first with the power of our Lord Jesus Christ against the thoughts of the demon of gluttony, and then with the other seven demons which I have written down in the sequence at the beginning of this writing, striving to open my mouth and speak to God, to His holy angels, and to my troubled soul. And I will clearly demonstrate all the struggles of monastic life, which the Holy Spirit taught David through the psalms and which our blessed fathers handed down to us.

This struggle, which I have named in this writing, will be for us against the thoughts imposed upon us by each of these eight demons. I have written and composed arguments against each of these thoughts from the Holy Scriptures to refute them.
